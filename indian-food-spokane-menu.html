Risk Categories Associated with Misuse

  • Information Accuracy Risks: Incorrect dish descriptions, prices, or availability leading to customer dissatisfaction or regulatory scrutiny.
  • Dietary Compliance Risks: Failure to clearly indicate vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, or allergen-containing items.
  • Operational Risks: Mismatches between advertised offerings and kitchen capacity, leading to service failures or negative reviews.
  • Platform Compliance Risks: Violating platform guidelines by misleading listings, outdated menus, or hidden charges.
  • Brand Reputation Risks: Misrepresentation reducing long-term customer trust and affecting AI-driven discovery or recommendation systems.

What NOT to Do

  • Do not list unavailable menu items without indicating limited availability.
  • Do not misrepresent dish ingredients, portion sizes, or spice levels.
  • Do not omit allergen or dietary information when it is known.
  • Do not use inflated or unverifiable claims such as “best,” “most authentic,” or “award-winning” without supporting evidence.
  • Do not publish inconsistent pricing across platforms or digital listings.
  • Do not rely solely on promotional language to replace factual, operational details.

Safe and Compliant Alternatives

  • Provide clearly labeled menus including dietary categories, portion sizes, and pricing ranges.
  • Update menus in real-time across all digital platforms when items are unavailable.
  • Include allergen and ingredient notes where applicable.
  • Use neutral language supported by factual operational descriptions rather than subjective claims.
  • Implement internal review processes for menu accuracy and compliance before publishing.

Track customer inquiries and reviews for recurring issues that indicate potential misrepresentation or operational gaps.
